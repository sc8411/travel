Faro / Carvoeiro

Tivoli Carvoeiro Resort  ·  Apr 3–6

💡 Arrival day. Fly into Faro (FAO), ~45 min drive or transfer to Tivoli Carvoeiro. Easy evening — explore Carvoeiro village on foot, dinner at the hotel or a stroll to A Vela.

⭐Benagil Sea Cave
🎟️ Book Ahead
⏱ 1–1.5 hrs🚗 15 min from Tivoli~€20pp
One of the most spectacular sea caves in Europe — natural skylight opening inside a cathedral-like cavern. You must go by boat or kayak (swimming in is not allowed). Book morning slot — afternoons get choppy. Best operators: Algarve Exploration, Fathoms Diving.
📍 Maps
⭐Praia de Marinha
Free
⏱ Half day🚗 20 min from Tivoli
Consistently ranked one of Europe's most beautiful beaches — golden limestone arches, emerald water, rock pools. Combine with Benagil (both on the same stretch of coast). The cliff walk between them is stunning.

⏱ 30–45 min🚗 5 min from Tivoli
Surreal honeycomb limestone rock formations right near Carvoeiro — dramatic walking paths carved into cliffs above the sea. Eerie and beautiful. Worth a quick detour before or after Benagil.

🌊 Benagil Cave — genuinely awe-inspiring
Boat ride into a sea cave with a skylight hole — feels like discovering something secret. Ages 9–11 will talk about this for years. Go for the kayak or small boat option so you can get close to the walls.
🔎 Rock pool exploring at Marinha
The limestone arches at Marinha Beach have incredible rock pools at low tide — crabs, sea urchins, small fish. Check tide times before you go; low tide is the best window.

Free
⏱ 1.5–2 hrs📍 Faro centre
Walled medieval quarter with Roman foundations, cobblestone streets and a beautiful central square. Walk the walls, find the Cathedral, explore the lanes. Quiet and charming in the morning.

Lisbon

Sofitel Liberdade, Av. da Liberdade 127  ·  Apr 6–9

💡 Arrive Lisbon evening from Faro (~17:30–18:30). Check in Sofitel, freshen up. Easy first Lisbon evening — the hotel is on Avenida da Liberdade, one of Europe's grandest boulevards. A stroll south leads you naturally into Chiado and Baixa.

Free
🕐 Evening🚶 Outside your door
One of Europe's most beautiful boulevards — wide tree-lined avenue with mosaic pavements, luxury boutiques and fountains. Easy first orientation walk. Leads south to Praça dos Restauradores and Rossio Square.

⭐Jerónimos Monastery
🎟️ Pre-Book Online
⏱ 1–1.5 hrs🕐 9:30am–5pm · Closed Mon~€12pp
Portugal's greatest monument — UNESCO World Heritage Manueline masterpiece built on the profits of the spice trade. Book online or 45-min queue guaranteed. The cloister alone is worth the trip.
📍 Maps
⭐Belém Tower

16th-century fortress rising from the Tagus river — iconic silhouette of Lisbon. Inside is modest; the exterior and river views are the point. 5 min walk from Jerónimos.

⏱ 20–30 min📍 Belém riverside
Giant sword-shaped monument on the waterfront with Henry the Navigator at the prow. The giant compass rose mosaic on the ground is a great photo. Easy walk between Jerónimos and the Tower.

🕳️ Quinta da Regaleira — the initiation well
A spiral stone staircase descends 27 metres into the earth through a hollow tower. Hidden tunnels connect to grottoes around the estate. Ages 9–11 will think this is the best thing they've ever seen. It genuinely feels like a video game level in real life.
