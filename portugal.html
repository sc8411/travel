💡 Arrive Lisbon ~11am from Faro. Check in to Sofitel, then dive straight into the city — start at Rossio Square, work through Baixa, ride the Santa Justa Lift, explore Carmo Convent, then hop Tram 28 up to Alfama and São Jorge Castle for sunset panoramas.
🚋 Use Funiculars & Trams — They save significant time on Lisbon's steep hills. Elevador da Glória goes up to Miradouro de São Pedro de Alcântara. Tram 28 is the classic route up to Alfama.
Rossio Square & Baixa Neighborhood
Free
🕐 Morning🚶 15 min from Sofitel
Lisbon's living room — famous wavy black-and-white mosaic pavement, cafes all around, the old Rossio train station. Explore the surrounding Baixa grid of elegant 18th-century streets.
📍 Maps
⭐Santa Justa Lift
⭐ Iconic
⏱ 20 min🕐 Daily 7am–11pm📍 Baixa · ~€5.30pp
A Gothic iron elevator built in 1902 that connects the lower Baixa with the upper Carmo neighborhood. Ride to the top terrace for sweeping views over the city's rooftops.
📍 Maps
⭐Carmo Convent (Convento do Carmo)
⭐ Must-Do
⏱ 45 min🕐 Mon–Sat 10am–7pm (Sun closed)📍 Top of Santa Justa · ~€5pp
One of Lisbon's most haunting landmarks — destroyed in the 1755 earthquake and deliberately left in ruins. The Gothic arches open up to the sky, making it one of the most photogenic spots in the city.
📍 Maps
⭐Tram 28 → Alfama District
⭐ Iconic
🚋 Afternoon📍 Baixa → Alfama
Jump on Tram 28 🚋 heading east — it climbs through the historic Alfama district. Unlike other Lisbon neighborhoods, Alfama survived the 1755 earthquake and remains a maze of narrow medieval streets. Get "lost" deliberately — it gives the best insight into local life.
📍 Maps
⭐São Jorge Castle
⭐ Best Views
⏱ 1 hr🕐 Daily 9am–9pm (Apr–Oct)📍 Alfama hilltop · ~€15pp
11th-century Moorish castle taken by King Afonso Henriques in 1147. Damaged in the 1755 earthquake, but the views are extraordinary — you can see most of Lisbon from up here. Visit in the afternoon to catch the golden-hour light on the city.

⏱ 30–45 min ride🎟️ ~€3pp each wayCatch near Sofitel on Rua da Glória
The most iconic tram ride in Europe — a century-old wooden tram crawling through impossibly narrow Alfama streets, grinding around corners with inches to spare. Rickety, slow and absolutely wonderful. Catch it early — gets packed by 10am.
